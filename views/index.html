Most every piece of the US federal government has an office called the Inspector General (IG).

An IG's job is to keep their part of the government honest and efficient through strong, independent oversight.

IGs produce a lot of work, but their reports are scattered all over the Internet. Some get the attention they deserve, and some don't.

It would be a shame for good oversight to go overlooked. We gather them in one place so you can freely search and subscribe to them.

Featured Report

July 28, 2014 — Waste and Mismanagement at the Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PDF)

The US Patent Office's board of appeals wasted $5 million since 2009 by paying people to do nothing, as the nation's patent backlog continued rising. Senior management knew of this and allowed it to continue.
